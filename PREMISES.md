## World Overview

| Aspect | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **World Name** | The Five Kingdoms (though only four are known) |
| **Theme** | A prisoner with a hidden crime escapes death row and washes ashore in a new land. Their past is a ghost that follows them—they can confess, hide, or embrace it. |
| **Visual Style** | "Theater of the Mind" — silhouettes for 90% of scenes, fully rendered AI images for major moments. |
| **Core Question** | *What do you do when you have a second chance at life—but you don't deserve it?* |
| **Name Origin** | Greek and Latin inspired names (Khionia = snow, Rhizoma = root, Arenia = sand/gold, Helos = marsh/swamp) |

## Kingdom 1: Khionia — Land of Endless Snow

### Geography

| Aspect | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Terrain** | High mountain peaks, deep valleys, frozen lakes, and vast snowfields. Glacial rivers cut through the rock. |
| **Climate** | Arctic/subarctic — bitterly cold year-round. Heavy snowfall for most of the year. Brief, cool summers with midnight sun. |
| **Elevation** | The highest of all kingdoms — the capital sits at the base of the tallest peak, Mount Boreas. |
| **Capital** | Chionopolis (Χιονόπολις) — "Snow City." Built on a sheltered plateau, surrounded by frozen waterfalls and ice cliffs. |
| **Key Features** | The Frozen Sea, The Ice Caves, The White Forest |
| **Natural Resources** | Silver, iron, ice (exported as a luxury), furs, rare cold-weather herbs |
| **Borders** | Northernmost kingdom. Bordered by mountains to the south and west, and the frozen sea to the north. |
| **Defenses** | Natural mountain barriers, impassable snowfields, avalanches. |
| **Motto** | "The snow remembers." |
| **Visual Palette** | Silhouettes: Black, white, grey, icy blue. Major Moments: Pale blue, silver, warm torchlight. |

### The Premise

**The Arrival:**
You wash ashore on a frozen beach. You stumble through the snow, half-dead, and find your way into the castle—not as a guest, but as a desperate stranger seeking warmth.

**The Inciting Incident:**
You slip on a wet staircase. You fall, foot-first, onto the royal chef, who was carrying soup to the royal family. The chef is knocked unconscious. The soup spills on **Princess Asteria's** dress.

She wants to kill you. The King stops her—he wants to give you a chance to explain.

**The Twist:**
The King's brother, **Lord Valerius**, walks in with "proof" that the chef was planning to poison the royal family. He claims you are a saviour who arrived by divine intervention.

The chef is dragged to the gallows. The King assigns you a role in the castle. You are now indebted to the King—and marked as an enemy by Princess Asteria.

**The Mystery:**
- The uncle arrived too conveniently. He's lying.
- The chef is innocent. He's a scapegoat.
- The soup was poisoned—by someone else.

### The Love Interest: Princess Asteria

| Aspect | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Role** | Princess of Khionia, heir to the throne |
| **Surface** | Cold, untouchable, utterly devoted to her people |
| **Reality** | Terrified. Her father is dying. Her brother was murdered—by her uncle. She knows, but she can't prove it. |
| **Arc** | From using you as a tool → seeing you as an equal → trusting you → loving you (or losing you) |
| **Secret** | She knows more about your crime than she lets on. The relic beneath Khionia's ice holds answers. |
| **Major Moment** | The first time you see her face—she removes her hood. She looks too young to carry so much grief. |

### Key NPCs

| Name | Role | Notes |
|------|------|-------|
| **King Leontios** | Ruler of Khionia | Dying, trusting, a good man who is blind to his brother's treachery |
| **Lord Valerius** | The King's brother | The real villain. He murdered the prince. He framed the chef. He knows your crime. |
| **The Royal Chef** | Unnamed | Innocent, framed, unconscious when dragged to the gallows |

### The Relic: Heart of the Frost

A shard of frozen star that fell to the world centuries ago. Buried beneath Khionia's ice. The player's crime is connected to it—they stole something from its temple.

## Kingdom 2: Rhizoma — Root of Life

### Geography

| Aspect | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Terrain** | Entirely underground. A vast network of caverns, root-tunnels, and underground rivers. The ceiling is a tangle of ancient roots, glowing with bioluminescent fungi. |
| **Climate** | Warm and humid year-round — geothermal vents keep the air warm. No seasons, no sunlight. |
| **Elevation** | Deep beneath the surface — the main city lies at least half a mile below ground. |
| **Capital** | Zōopolis (Ζωόπολις) — "City of Life." Built around the Heart-Root. |
| **Key Features** | The Heart-Root, The Sap-Rivers, The Fungal Forests, The Deep Tunnels |
| **Natural Resources** | Fungi, amber, root-essence, medicinal herbs, rare minerals |
| **Borders** | Beneath all other kingdoms. Roots stretch under every surface kingdom. |
| **Defenses** | The root-tunnels are a labyrinth—only the Root-People know the safe paths. |
| **Motto** | "From roots, all things." |
| **Visual Palette** | Silhouettes: Black, deep brown, muted gold. Major Moments: Bioluminescent green, amber, golden light. |

### The Premise

**The Arrival:**
You are sucked into an underground current after the kraken attack. You wake up tangled in massive roots, disoriented and half-drowned.

**The First Encounter:**
A girl is picking glowing mushrooms nearby—**Thalloa**. She observes you cautiously. She tells you to follow her. She'll get you a job from her master.

**The Setup:**
You end up as the **assistant to a merchant**. You learn about Rhizoma's goods—fungi, amber, root-essence, medicinal herbs. The work is simple, repetitive, and safe.

**The Change:**
You are the first person to trade **differently**. Using negotiation techniques, product knowledge, and strategies no one in Rhizoma has ever seen—from your old life, from other kingdoms, from memory fragments. The merchant notices. The guild takes interest.

**The Complication:**
You accidentally trade a **poisonous mushroom** to a noble family. The noble falls ill. You're blamed. Thalloa begs her master to protect you. He does—but now you owe him your life. He wants to use your skills for his own ambitions.

**The Question:**
You've changed Rhizoma with a single trade. What happens when you change something *more*?

### The Love Interest: Thalloa

| Aspect | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Role** | Root-gatherer, lowest caste, assistant to the merchant |
| **Surface** | Quiet, cautious, used to being invisible |
| **Reality** | She can hear the tree. She's always been able to. She thought everyone could. |
| **Arc** | From root-gatherer → rebel → healer → leader |
| **Secret** | The tree speaks to her. It's been telling her that you are the key to saving Rhizoma. |
| **Major Moment** | The first time she sees the sun—tears streaming down her face, overwhelmed by wonder and grief. |

### Key NPCs

| Name | Role | Notes |
|------|------|-------|
| **Master Merchant** | The merchant | He saves you. Now he owns you. He wants to use your skills for his ambitions. |
| **The Canopy Lord** | Ruler of the canopy-dwellers | He's draining the tree's essence to keep his gardens alive. The rot is his doing. |

### The Relic: Heartwood Seed

A seed from the first tree, the one that gave birth to all life. Buried deep in the roots, beneath Thalloa's village. The canopy lord wants it.

## Kingdom 3: Arenia — Land of Gold

### Geography

| Aspect | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Terrain** | Coastal desert. Golden sand dunes, rocky cliffs, sweeping beaches. Two major rivers flow from inland mountains to the sea. |
| **Climate** | Hot and dry. Coastal areas cooled by trade winds. Monsoon rains in late summer. |
| **Elevation** | Sea-level along the coast, rising to low hills inland. |
| **Capital** | Aureapolis (Αὐρεάπολις) — "City of Gold." A sprawling port city built around the estuary where the Golden and Silver rivers meet the sea. |
| **Key Features** | The Golden River, The Silver River, The Twin Veins, The Salt Flats, The Golden Dunes |
| **Natural Resources** | Gold, silver, salt, fish, ivory, cotton |
| **Borders** | Southern coastline. Bordered by desert to the north and east, and the sea to the south and west. |
| **Defenses** | The desert itself is a barrier. Armies must cross miles of harsh, waterless sand. |
| **Motto** | "Gold from sand." |
| **Visual Palette** | Silhouettes: Sand, gold, faded orange. Major Moments: Sunset red, golden hour glow, deep shadows. |

### The Premise

**The Arrival:**
You wash up on a golden shore—sand so fine it looks like powdered sunlight. You're half-dead, face-down, barely breathing.

**The First Encounter:**
She finds you first. A figure in battered leather, sand-caked boots, a sword at her hip, and cold eyes.

**Aurea** — The Falcon of the West. She didn't come to save you. She came to loot you.

**The Convincing:**
You breathe. She holds you at sword point. You talk fast—ridiculous reasons fall out of your mouth. She laughs. It's sharp and hollow.

**The Collar:**
She snaps an **iron slave collar** around your neck. It's heavy, cold, and degrading. You are her property. You carry her supplies. You hold her horse. The collar is a constant reminder: *you belong to her.*

**The Unspoken Truth:**
You are the first person she hasn't killed in years. The first person she's talked to for more than a few words. The first person who's made her laugh. She won't say it—but the collar is also her way of keeping you close.

**The Future:**
You are a slave. But you are also the only companion the Falcon of the West has ever had. The slow rise is inevitable. You'll earn her respect. You'll earn your freedom. And when the collar comes off... it will mean something.

### The Love Interest: Aurea (The Falcon of the West)

| Aspect | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Role** | Mercenary, feared across the desert |
| **Surface** | Cold, sharp, deadly. She kills without hesitation. |
| **Reality** | Lonely. She's been alone for years. You are the first person she's kept alive. |
| **Arc** | From captor → reluctant protector → trusted partner → something more |
| **Secret** | She's running from something too. A past she's trying to outrun. |
| **Major Moment** | The first time she laughs—genuinely, without cynicism—and you see her face soften. |

### Key NPCs

| Name | Role | Notes |
|------|------|-------|
| **The Merchant** | Wealthy slave trader | You work for him. He's corrupt. He knows something about your past. |

### The Relic: The Golden Vein

A legendary vein of gold buried deep beneath the Salt Flats. It's said to be cursed—anyone who mines it goes mad.

## Kingdom 4: Helos — Land of Darkness

### Geography

| Aspect | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Terrain** | Vast, mist-choked swamp. Dark water, twisted trees, hanging moss, and bioluminescent fungi. |
| **Climate** | Humid, warm, and perpetually misty. Rain is frequent. Fog rolls in every evening. |
| **Elevation** | Built vertically — the Deep is at swamp level, the Crown is on the highest hill. |
| **Capital** | Phosphopolis (Φωσφόπολις) — "City of Light." Built on the tallest hill, rising above the mist. |
| **Key Features** | The Deep, The Mist, The Black Water, The Causeways, The Whispering Reeds |
| **Natural Resources** | Medicinal herbs, reed-paper, fish, amphibian leather, swamp-grain |
| **Borders** | Surrounded by swamp on all sides. Nearest dry land is days away. |
| **Defenses** | The swamp itself—armies don't march through swamps. |
| **Motto** | "From darkness, light." |
| **Visual Palette** | Silhouettes: Black, deep green, purple-grey. Major Moments: Bioluminescent purple, swamp-green, eerie light. |

### The Premise

**The Arrival:**
You wash up in a marsh. Alligators surround you. You're too weak to fight.

**The First Encounter:**
A bolt of purple light shoots past your head. The alligators scatter. A girl emerges from the mist—**Sidea**. She's young. Barely out of her teens. She saved you—not out of kindness, but because she wants a test subject.

**The Binding Spell (Not a Physical Collar):**
She waves her staff. A binding spell wraps around your wrist—a **curse**, not a collar. It's glowing, cold, and whispers in your ear at night. It's not a physical chain—it's a magical compulsion. You can't leave her side. You can't hurt her. But unlike the collar, this one might actually kill you if she doesn't learn to control it.

> *"There. Now you can't leave. Don't worry—it's not permanent. Probably. I haven't figured out how to undo it yet."*

**The Magic:**
Sidea is an *enthusiastic* witch. She knows the words, but not the consequences. Her spells go hilariously wrong:

- Healing a wound → Your arm turns into a tentacle for three hours.
- Curing a fever → You start glowing in the dark—permanently.
- Making you stronger → You can only speak in rhymes for a week.

**The Bond:**
She's lonely. She's been alone in the swamp for years. You're not just a test subject—you're the first person she's had to talk to in a long time. She doesn't say it, but the binding spell wasn't just to keep you close. It was to keep you *with* her.

**The Future:**
You're trapped. You're mutated. You're her test subject. But when the swamp's true dangers reveal themselves, she'll risk everything to save you—even if her magic makes it worse before it gets better. And when the binding spell eventually breaks... will you stay?

### The Love Interest: Sidea

| Aspect | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Role** | Young witch, self-taught, inexperienced |
| **Surface** | Enthusiastic, chaotic, eager to learn |
| **Reality** | Lonely, desperate for connection, terrified of failure |
| **Arc** | From incompetent witch → learning to control her power → finding confidence → protecting someone she cares about |
| **Secret** | She's not just bad at magic—she's *afraid* of it. Her power terrifies her. |
| **Major Moment** | The first time she casts a spell *correctly*—and it changes everything. |

### Key NPCs

| Name | Role | Notes |
|------|------|-------|
| **Alligators** | Predators | They almost killed you. She saved you from them. |
| **The Voice** | ??? | Something in the swamp is whispering to her. Something old. |

### The Relic: The Whispering Heart

A heart-shaped stone buried in the deepest part of the swamp. It pulses with dark energy. Sidea doesn't know it exists—but it's calling to her.

## The Forgotten Fifth Kingdom

There is a fifth kingdom, but no one remembers its name. It was lost—or destroyed—centuries ago. Its ruins are scattered across the other kingdoms.

**The Mystery:**
- What happened to it?
- Was it destroyed by the other kingdoms?
- Did it destroy itself?
- Is there a survivor?

**The Hook:**
The player might find pieces of its history. A ruin in the Ice Caves. A carving in the Deep Tunnels. A relic in the Salt Flats. A whisper in the swamp.

## The Player's Crime

**Fixed. Hidden. Central to the story. Ambiguous by design.**

The player is not a monster. Whatever they did, it was not born of pure evil. It was a mistake, a moment of weakness, a choice made in desperation. The player will discover the truth gradually—and when they do, they will have to decide: confess, hide, or embrace it.

| Possibility | Tone |
|-------------|------|
| **Regicide** | Political, tragic, heavy — you killed a king, but perhaps there was a reason. |
| **Betrayal** | Moral grey, haunting — you sold out your people, but perhaps you were trying to save someone. |
| **Heresy** | Spiritual, philosophical — you killed a god, but perhaps the god deserved it. |
| **Accident** | Tragic, human — you caused a death, but you never meant to. |
| **Innocence** | The twist — you were framed. You never committed the crime. |

**The key is ambiguity.** The crime is never fully explained in a single scene. It surfaces through nightmares, memories, NPC reactions, and the player's own choices. The player is not a monster—but they have done something they must answer for.

## The Prologue

**Setting:** A cargo ship. You are a prisoner. The chains are cold. The guards are silent.

**The Incident:** The kraken attacks. The ship breaks apart. You are thrown into the sea.

**The Escape:** You choose how to survive. Your choices determine which kingdom you wash up in.

**The Result:** You wash up in one of four kingdoms, with nothing but the clothes on your back and a past you can't remember—but can't escape.
